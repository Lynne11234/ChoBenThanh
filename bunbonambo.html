BÚN BÒ NAM BỘ

Northern style Beef Noodle Salad

DIFFICULTY:
Ingredients
  • Beef tenderloin: 500g
  • Noodles (Bún): 1kg
  • Roasted peanuts: 100g
  • Chili: 2 pieces
  • Garlic: 2 cloves
  • Lemongrass: 3 stalks
  • Shallots: 7 pieces
  • Lime: 1 fruit
  • Fresh herbs: basil, perilla, lettuce, coriander, bean sprouts, cucumber. You can add tomatoes or sliced papaya for more variety in the veggies.
  • Seasonings: fish sauce, sugar, salt, cooking oil, seasoning powder, oyster sauce, soy sauce, ground pepper.
Instructions

Prepare the Ingredients:

  • Wash the beef, drain it, and slice it thinly. Marinate with 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1 teaspoon of oyster sauce, 1 teaspoon of seasoning powder, a pinch of ground pepper, and mix well. Let it marinate for about 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  • Clean and remove the roots and old leaves of the herbs. Soak them in diluted salt water, wash thoroughly, and drain. After that, cut the herbs into small pieces.
  • Blanch the bean sprouts in hot water for 1-2 minutes, then drain. Wash the cucumber and slice it into thin strips.
  • Peel and thinly slice the shallots, crush and finely chop the garlic, chili, and lemongrass.
  • Crush the roasted peanuts, cut the lime in half, squeeze the juice, and remove the seeds.

Fry the Shallots until Golden:

  • Heat oil in a pan. Once the oil is hot, add the thinly sliced shallots.
  • Stir continuously until the shallots turn golden brown, then turn off the heat and transfer the fried shallots to a bowl.

Stir-fry the Beef to Retain Its Natural Sweetness

  • In the same pan used to fry the shallots, add a little more oil and heat it over high heat.
  • Add half of the chopped garlic and lemongrass into the pan and stir-fry until fragrant.
  • Add the beef to the pan, stirring quickly to prevent releasing too much juice.
  • When the beef is slightly cooked, turn off the heat and transfer it to a plate.
  • Tip: Use a non-stick pan and stir-fry quickly to keep the beef tender, retain its natural sweetness, and avoid it becoming tough.

Make the Dipping Sauce:

  • In a bowl, add 4 tablespoons of good-quality fish sauce, 4 tablespoons of sugar, 1 tablespoon of warm water, and the juice from 1 lime.
  • Stir the mixture with a spoon until the sugar dissolves and the ingredients blend together.
  • Add the chopped garlic and chili, then stir again to combine everything.
  • Tip: You can adjust the seasoning to your liking, balancing the salty, sweet, sour, and spicy flavors to suit your taste.
Serve and enjoy the dish!
  • After completing the steps above, place the noodles into a bowl.
  • Add the prepared fresh herbs on top, followed by the cooked beef, roasted peanuts, and fried shallots.
  • Finally, pour the dipping sauce over everything, mix well, and enjoy.
  • Tip: If the noodles have been stored in the fridge, you should blanch them in hot water for 1-2 minutes, then drain them thoroughly before serving.
